# How it works (sorta)
- Runs a speedtest on a randomized timer between 5 and 60 minutes
- Uses setTimeout chaining to avoid fixed intervals
- Executes directly on speedtest.net page
- Logs results to console or local storage (if applicable)

# Explanation of what this userscript is doing

Speedtest.net is a great tool to use to help keep your ISP fair and balanced™. What my autistic ass means by that is, if you are paying for 1000mbps up/down, with this tool over the course of days/months/years you ***CAN*** show data to prove your claims of your ISP dropping the ball (if they are, my ISP rocks). You can also use this userscript as a tool to just keep extra data about how your internet speeds fluctuate within your given neighborhood so you can track peak uptime, most congestive times, etc. Data is data, and this tool is only helping you automate the process of having your speedtests run independantly of yourself. 

There shouldn't be any issues with this code, but if for whatever reason Speedtest.net decides to throw a monkey wrench in my whole plan here, shoot me an issue [*here*](https://github.com/NoahBK/Speedtest.netButAutomated/issues).
